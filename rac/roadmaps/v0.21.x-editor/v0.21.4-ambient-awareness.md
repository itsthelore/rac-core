# RAC v0.21.4 — Ambient Corpus Awareness

## Status

Planned

## Context

Through v0.21.3 the extension reacts to the file in front of you. A maintainer
also needs a sense of the whole corpus at a glance — is it healthy, what is
broken — without running a command or opening each file. This release adds
ambient, always-on awareness, drawn from `rac review` and `rac validate <dir>`
(ADR-063).

## Outcomes

- The corpus's health is visible at a glance and stays current as artifacts change.
- The Problems panel reflects the whole corpus, not only the documents that
  happen to be open.

## Initiatives

### Initiative 1 — Status-bar health indicator

A status-bar item showing the corpus health score and valid/invalid counts from
`rac review`, updated on a debounced change, with click-through to open the
review (or the Problems panel).

### Initiative 2 — Workspace-wide diagnostics

On activation (and on a debounced corpus change), run `rac validate <dir>` and
populate the Problems panel for every artifact, so issues in unopened files are
visible. Per-file results carry line-anchored issues, so diagnostics land on the
right lines.

## Constraints

- Thin client only (ADR-063): health from `rac review`, diagnostics from
  `rac validate <dir>`.
- Workspace-wide runs are debounced and scoped so they do not thrash on every
  keystroke; they reconcile with the per-file diagnostics from v0.21.0/v0.21.1.

## Non-Goals

- A separate dashboard/report view beyond the status bar and Problems panel
  (the graph view is v0.21.5).
- Blocking the editor on corpus scans; everything is async and advisory.

## Implementation Contract

- The status-bar item reflects `rac review`'s health score and validity counts
  and updates on change.
- Workspace-wide diagnostics come from `rac validate <dir>` and merge cleanly
  with live per-file diagnostics (no duplicate or stale entries).

## Success Measures

- The status bar shows the same health score as `rac review` and updates as
  artifacts change.
- An invalid artifact that is not open still appears in the Problems panel.
- Fixing it clears the entry on the next debounced scan.

## Risks

- Corpus-scan cost and diagnostic churn on large repos. Mitigated by debouncing,
  caching (v0.21.6), and reconciling workspace scans with live per-file results.

## Assumptions

- `rac review` and `rac validate <dir>` are fast enough, with debouncing, for an
  interactive status indicator and Problems-panel population.
