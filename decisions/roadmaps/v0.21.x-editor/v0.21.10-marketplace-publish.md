# RAC v0.21.10 — Marketplace & OpenVSX Publish

## Status

Planned

## Context

The extension is packaged as a `.vsix` (v0.21.6) but unpublished, so its reach
is zero and every other improvement is improving software no one runs. Sideload-
ing a `.vsix` across a team is a non-starter for rollout — it dies on the first
update. Publishing is the single highest-leverage unblock. Before going public,
the Explorer webview's `postMessage` bridge needs CSP and origin hardening; a
published extension shipping an unhardened bridge is a real security finding.

## Outcomes

- One-click install and auto-update from the VS Code Marketplace and OpenVSX.
- A repeatable, tag-driven release rather than hand-packaging.
- A hardened, publish-ready webview.

## Initiatives

### Initiative 1 — Automated release pipeline

A tag-triggered CI workflow that builds, runs the v0.21.9 test gate, then
`vsce publish` + `ovsx publish` — publish is the last step, reachable only on
green.

### Initiative 2 — Webview hardening

A strict `Content-Security-Policy` and validated message origin on the Explorer
webview before first publish.

### Initiative 3 — Marketplace listing

README, the corpus-graph render, a short walkthrough, keywords/categories — and
the no-telemetry / offline posture stated as a selling point.

## Constraints

- Publish only behind the green test gate (v0.21.9).
- No telemetry.
- The publisher account and tokens are a human-owned step, called out rather
  than automated.

## Non-Goals

- Paid or closed distribution.
- Bundling the `rac` engine — provisioning is v0.21.11.

## Implementation Contract

- A tagged CI workflow gates on tests then publishes to Marketplace + OpenVSX.
- The webview ships a strict CSP and an origin-checked bridge.
- The listing states the no-telemetry/offline posture.

## Success Measures

- Installing from Marketplace/OpenVSX works and auto-updates.
- A tag produces a published release with no manual packaging.
- The webview enforces CSP and rejects unexpected message origins.

## Risks

- An untested bundle shipping faster and wider. Mitigated by the v0.21.9 gate.
- Publisher-account/secret handling. Human-owned and documented.

## Assumptions

- A publisher account and tokens are available (human), and the v0.21.9 gate
  exists.
